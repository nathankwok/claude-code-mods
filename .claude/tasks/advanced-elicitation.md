# Task: Advanced Elicitation Techniques

## Purpose
Apply deep dive elicitation techniques to explore complex problem spaces, uncover hidden requirements, and generate sophisticated insights through systematic analysis and creative exploration.

## Task Overview
This task focuses on intensive elicitation using advanced BMAD techniques for:
- Complex problem decomposition and analysis
- Hidden requirement discovery
- Stakeholder need clarification
- Technical constraint exploration
- Innovation opportunity identification

## Prerequisites
- Basic brainstorming session completed OR clear problem definition
- Access to stakeholder information and project context
- User availability for focused, intensive exploration session
- Understanding of domain constraints and technical environment

## Instructions

### Phase 1: Problem Deep Dive

1. **Root Cause Analysis (Advanced Application)**
   ```
   Multi-Layer Analysis:
   - Layer 1: Surface symptoms and immediate problems
   - Layer 2: Underlying system issues and process breakdowns
   - Layer 3: Organizational and cultural factors
   - Layer 4: Market and external forces
   - Layer 5: Fundamental assumptions and mental models
   
   For each layer, apply:
   - 5 Whys technique with multiple branches
   - Cause-and-effect mapping with feedback loops
   - System dynamics analysis for complex interactions
   - Assumption validation and challenge
   ```

2. **Problem Reframing (Systematic Approach)**
   ```
   Generate Multiple Problem Statements:
   - Technical Problem: Focus on system/technology aspects
   - User Problem: Focus on end-user experience and needs
   - Business Problem: Focus on organizational and market concerns
   - Process Problem: Focus on workflow and operational issues
   - Strategic Problem: Focus on long-term vision and competitive position
   
   For each reframing:
   - Define problem scope and boundaries
   - Identify key stakeholders affected
   - Map potential solution approaches
   - Assess complexity and implementation challenges
   ```

### Phase 2: Stakeholder Perspective Analysis

3. **Advanced Perspective Shifting**
   ```
   Comprehensive Stakeholder Mapping:
   
   Primary Stakeholders:
   - End Users: Direct product/service users
   - Customers: Those who pay for the solution
   - Internal Team: Developers, designers, managers
   - Decision Makers: Those who approve/reject solutions
   
   Secondary Stakeholders:
   - Partners and vendors
   - Regulatory bodies
   - Competitors and market forces
   - Future stakeholders (growth scenarios)
   
   For each stakeholder:
   - Define their specific needs and pain points
   - Identify their success criteria and metrics
   - Map their constraints and limitations
   - Understand their decision-making process
   - Explore their alternative options and preferences
   ```

4. **Stakeholder Conflict Resolution**
   ```
   Identify and Resolve Conflicting Requirements:
   - Map contradictory needs between stakeholders
   - Analyze trade-offs and compromise opportunities
   - Explore win-win solutions through creative problem solving
   - Prioritize requirements based on stakeholder influence and impact
   - Design solutions that address core needs while managing conflicts
   ```

### Phase 3: Constraint and Opportunity Analysis

5. **Advanced Constraint Removal**
   ```
   Systematic Constraint Analysis:
   
   Technical Constraints:
   - Technology limitations and dependencies
   - Performance and scalability requirements
   - Security and compliance requirements
   - Integration and compatibility needs
   
   Resource Constraints:
   - Budget limitations and cost considerations
   - Timeline and deadline pressures
   - Team size and skill availability
   - Infrastructure and tool limitations
   
   Business Constraints:
   - Market conditions and competitive pressures
   - Regulatory and legal requirements
   - Company policies and strategic direction
   - Customer expectations and commitments
   
   For each constraint:
   - Explore scenarios with constraint removed
   - Identify workarounds and alternative approaches
   - Assess constraint flexibility and negotiability
   - Design solutions that minimize constraint impact
   ```

6. **Innovation Opportunity Identification**
   ```
   Creative Opportunity Mapping:
   - Technology innovation opportunities (new tools, frameworks, approaches)
   - Process innovation opportunities (workflow improvements, automation)
   - User experience innovations (interface, interaction, accessibility)
   - Business model innovations (monetization, value delivery)
   - Market positioning innovations (differentiation, niche targeting)
   
   For each opportunity:
   - Assess innovation potential and impact
   - Evaluate feasibility and risk factors
   - Map resource requirements and dependencies
   - Design pilot approaches for validation
   ```

### Phase 4: Advanced Creative Techniques

7. **Extended SCAMPER Analysis**
   ```
   Apply SCAMPER to Different Solution Aspects:
   
   Architecture Level:
   - Substitute: Alternative architectural patterns
   - Combine: Integration with existing systems
   - Adapt: Lessons from similar architectures
   - Modify: Scale considerations and performance
   - Put to other uses: Multi-purpose design opportunities
   - Eliminate: Simplification and minimalism
   - Reverse: Alternative flow and process directions
   
   Feature Level:
   - Apply SCAMPER to each major feature
   - Cross-pollinate ideas between features
   - Explore feature combinations and interactions
   - Consider feature elimination and consolidation
   
   User Experience Level:
   - Alternative interaction patterns
   - Combined interface elements
   - Adapted patterns from other domains
   - Modified user journeys and flows
   ```

8. **Analogical Thinking (Deep Analysis)**
   ```
   Multi-Domain Analogy Exploration:
   
   Technical Analogies:
   - Similar systems in other industries
   - Analogous algorithms and data structures
   - Parallel architectural patterns
   - Comparable performance optimization approaches
   
   Process Analogies:
   - Similar workflows in different contexts
   - Analogous problem-solving approaches
   - Comparable optimization strategies
   - Parallel coordination mechanisms
   
   Natural Analogies:
   - Biological systems and processes
   - Physical systems and laws
   - Ecological relationships and balances
   - Evolutionary adaptation strategies
   
   For each analogy:
   - Map structural similarities
   - Identify functional parallels
   - Extract applicable principles
   - Adapt solutions to current context
   ```

### Phase 5: Technical Deep Dive

9. **Technical Architecture Elicitation**
   ```
   Comprehensive Technical Analysis:
   
   System Requirements:
   - Functional requirements and capabilities
   - Non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability)
   - Integration requirements and dependencies
   - Deployment and operational requirements
   
   Technology Stack Considerations:
   - Programming languages and frameworks
   - Database and storage solutions
   - Infrastructure and hosting options
   - Third-party services and APIs
   
   Design Pattern Analysis:
   - Applicable architectural patterns
   - Design patterns for specific challenges
   - Anti-patterns to avoid
   - Pattern combinations and interactions
   
   Risk and Challenge Assessment:
   - Technical risks and mitigation strategies
   - Implementation challenges and complexity
   - Scalability and performance concerns
   - Security and compliance considerations
   ```

10. **Implementation Strategy Elicitation**
    ```
    Detailed Implementation Planning:
    
    Phase Breakdown:
    - Logical implementation phases and dependencies
    - Minimum viable product (MVP) definition
    - Incremental feature delivery strategy
    - Validation and feedback incorporation points
    
    Resource Planning:
    - Skill requirements and team composition
    - Timeline estimates and milestone planning
    - Budget considerations and cost optimization
    - Risk management and contingency planning
    
    Quality Assurance:
    - Testing strategies and coverage requirements
    - Code review and quality standards
    - Performance monitoring and optimization
    - User acceptance and validation criteria
    ```

## Advanced Facilitation Techniques

### Deep Questioning Strategies
```
Question Types for Different Exploration Goals:

Clarification Questions:
- "What do you mean when you say...?"
- "Can you give me a specific example of...?"
- "How does this relate to...?"

Assumption Questions:
- "What assumptions are we making about...?"
- "What if the opposite were true?"
- "How do we know that...?"

Evidence Questions:
- "What evidence supports this conclusion?"
- "How could we validate this assumption?"
- "What data would change your mind about...?"

Perspective Questions:
- "How might [stakeholder] view this differently?"
- "What are the long-term implications of...?"
- "What are we not considering here?"

Implication Questions:
- "If this solution succeeds, what else changes?"
- "What are the unintended consequences of...?"
- "How does this impact our other objectives?"
```

### Insight Synthesis Methods
```
Pattern Recognition:
- Identify recurring themes across different exploration areas
- Map connections between seemingly unrelated insights
- Recognize system-level patterns and relationships
- Highlight breakthrough insights that challenge assumptions

Insight Validation:
- Cross-check insights against stakeholder feedback
- Validate assumptions with data and evidence
- Test insights through thought experiments
- Seek disconfirming evidence and alternative explanations

Insight Organization:
- Group insights by theme and relationship
- Prioritize insights by potential impact and feasibility
- Map insights to specific problem areas and solutions
- Create insight hierarchies from tactical to strategic
```
